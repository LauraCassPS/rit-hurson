Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Divorce With Dignity ("we", "us") is operated by Rita Hurson, based in Dublin, Ireland. This policy explains what personal information this website collects, how it is used, and the rights you have over it.

Who is responsible for your data

Rita Hurson is the data controller for any personal information collected through this website. Any questions about this policy or your data can be sent to rita26.acp@gmail.com.

What we collect

We only collect personal information that you choose to give us. This happens in two ways:

  • The contact form on this website, which asks for your name, email address, and message.
  • Direct contact you initiate by email, WhatsApp, or LinkedIn, which may include whatever information you choose to share with us.

We do not use cookies, analytics, or any tracking technology on this website, and we do not collect personal information automatically just by you visiting the site.

How we use it

We use the information you provide only to respond to your enquiry and, if you choose to go ahead, to provide our services to you. We do not use your information for marketing without your separate consent, and we never sell or rent your information to third parties.

Our legal basis for processing

We process your information on the basis of consent (you choosing to contact us) and, where you become a client, to take steps towards and perform a contract for services with you.

Who else sees your information

The contact form on this website is processed by Formspree, a third-party form-handling service, which delivers your message to us by email. Formspree's own privacy policy governs how it handles data in transit.

This website loads typefaces from Google Fonts. Depending on your browser and location, this may involve your device connecting to Google's servers to download font files, which can share your IP address with Google. We do not control this and encourage you to review Google's privacy policy if you have concerns.

Beyond this, we do not share your information with any other third party except where required by law.

How long we keep it

We keep contact form submissions and correspondence only for as long as necessary to respond to your enquiry, or, if you become a client, for as long as needed to provide our services and meet our professional and legal record-keeping obligations. After that, it is deleted.

Your rights

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:

  • Ask what personal information we hold about you, and get a copy of it.
  • Ask us to correct information that is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Ask us to delete your information, where there is no legal reason for us to keep it.
  • Ask us to restrict or object to how we use your information.
  • Receive your information in a portable format, where technically feasible.

To exercise any of these rights, contact rita26.acp@gmail.com. You also have the right to complain to Ireland's Data Protection Commission at dataprotection.ie (opens in a new tab) if you believe your data has been mishandled.

Confidentiality

Beyond standard data protection law, discretion is central to how Rita works with clients. Details of your situation shared with us in the course of our work together are treated as confidential and are not discussed outside that professional relationship, except where we are legally required to disclose them.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date at the top.
